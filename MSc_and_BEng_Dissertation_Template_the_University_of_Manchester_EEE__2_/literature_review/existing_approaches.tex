\subsection{Existing Optimisation Approaches}

Zorbas et al.~\cite{zorbas2025revisiting} propose a Mixed Integer
Linear Programming framework for optimal SF allocation. MILP scales
exponentially with binary SF variables, becoming intractable for
$N > 20$ sensors, and assumes channel conditions are known and
stationary at solve time, an assumption that fails continuously as a
mobile UAV and the EMA-ADR loop propagate outdated RSSI estimates.
The authors themselves conclude that reinforcement learning is the
appropriate tool for the dynamic case.

Xiong et al.~\cite{xiong2023flyinglora} introduce FlyingLoRa, which
minimises energy by jointly optimising the UAV's 3D trajectory and
transmission scheduling offline, given full knowledge of sensor
locations and link budgets before the mission. FlyingLoRa omits ADR
dynamics (treating each sensor's rate as fixed) and does not model
the Capture Effect, and therefore cannot exploit the
positioning-induced SF diversity that the protocol-aware DRL agent
learns to generate. The channel-geometry and energy-consumption
assumptions that underpin this work follow the UAV-communications
tutorials by Zeng et al.~\cite{zeng2016wireless},
Mozaffari et al.~\cite{mozaffari2019tutorial}, and
Zeng and Zhang~\cite{zeng2017energy}.

At the intersection of DRL and UAV communications, Challita et
al.~\cite{challita2018interference} propose a deep RL framework for
interference-aware path planning of cellular-connected UAVs,
demonstrating that DRL agents can learn collision-avoidance behaviour
that static planners cannot replicate. Nemer et
al.~\cite{nemer2022energyefficient} apply a distributed actor--critic
approach to maximise coverage fairness for multi-UAV networks,
confirming Jain's Fairness Index as an appropriate target metric.
Neither study encounters the non-stationary MAC-layer dynamics
(EMA-ADR, duty cycles, Capture Effect) that make the LoRaWAN setting
structurally harder. The DQN versus policy-gradient algorithm
selection argument for this specific reward landscape is in
Appendix~\ref{app:extended_methodology}.
