\subsection{Sim-to-Real Perturbation Results}
\label{app:sim_to_real}

This appendix reports a test-time robustness sweep in which the trained
DQN and Relational RL policies are evaluated under three classes of
deliberate sim-to-real gap: GPS position noise on the UAV observation,
elevated log-normal shadowing variance on the LoRa channel, and a
constant additive wind-drift bias on the UAV displacement. Neither
policy is retrained. All runs use a $200\times200$ grid, $N=20$
sensors, deterministic (argmax) action selection, and $10$ seeds per
cell (seed range $2000$--$2009$); the raw per-episode results are in
\texttt{baseline\_results/sim\_to\_real/robustness\_raw.csv} and the
driver is \texttt{sim\_to\_real\_sweep.py}. Each axis is perturbed
independently with the remaining two held at their training defaults
(GPS~noise $=0$, shadowing $\sigma=4$\,dB, wind drift $=0$).

The headline finding is \emph{absence of degradation}: both policies
retain near-complete coverage across every perturbation magnitude,
and the Relational RL policy retains its structural advantage
(higher reward, lower wall collisions, lower edge-step occupancy)
throughout.

On GPS position noise, additive Gaussian noise with standard deviation $\sigma \in
\{0,5,10,20\}$ grid units is applied to the UAV's observed position
before each observation is assembled; the true position used for
physics is unperturbed. At $\sigma=20$ (approximately a $\pm$200\,m
observation error on a $200\times200$ grid at $\approx 10$\,m per
unit, i.e.\ 10\% of the grid span) DQN coverage drops only from
$99.0\%$ to $98.0\%$ and reward remains within the seed-to-seed
envelope of the baseline ($1.78$ vs.\ $1.80 \times 10^6$; std
$\approx 0.24 \times 10^6$). The Relational RL policy returns
$100.0\%$ coverage at every magnitude and its edge-step occupancy
in fact \emph{falls} as the GPS noise grows (30\% $\to$ 15\%),
consistent with noisy positional self-reports pushing the agent away
from the grid perimeter.

For log-normal shadowing, the standard deviation on every sensor's RSSI is elevated
from the training default of $4$\,dB to $\{6, 8, 10\}$\,dB. Both
policies retain $100\%$ coverage across the sweep; the DQN's reward
actually \emph{increases} monotonically with shadowing magnitude
($1.80 \to 2.09 \times 10^6$), because higher channel variance
occasionally produces RSSI realisations that decode packets the
baseline model would have dropped, while the EMA-ADR filter ($\lambda=0.1$) suppresses the corresponding
SF over-reaction.
The Relational RL policy stays within $\pm 3.5\%$ of its baseline
reward throughout, confirming that its advantage is not an artefact
of a specific channel variance regime.

For wind drift, a probabilistic additive displacement (a second movement step in the
commanded direction with probability $\min(0.95,\, \text{drift}/2)$)
is injected into the step dynamics, modelling a steady-state wind
bias. At drift $= 2.0$ cells/step the Relational RL policy loses
approximately $0.5\%$ of baseline reward and its wall-collision count
stays below four per episode; the DQN wall-collision count rises from
$62$ to $86$ at the lightest drift ($0.5$) but then falls again at
higher magnitudes, which is consistent with the drift occasionally
pushing the edge-hugging agent \emph{into} the wall and occasionally
\emph{along} it.

One caveat applies to the path-loss exponent axis. A fourth axis was planned to perturb the log-distance path-loss
exponent $n \in \{3.4, 3.8, 4.2, 4.6\}$, but the Two-Ray ground
reflection model implemented in
\texttt{iot\_sensors.py:\allowbreak calculate\_rssi()}
uses hardcoded slope coefficients (20 and 40~dB/decade in the
near- and far-field regimes respectively) rather than a parametric
exponent; the attribute written by the sweep harness is therefore
inert, and the four path-loss cells are bit-identical. The PLE axis
is consequently omitted from Table~\ref{tab:sim_to_real_full}. A
parametric log-distance implementation is identified as future work
(Section~\ref{sec:future_work}).

Taken together, over the three working axes ($3 \times 4 = 12$ perturbation cells
$\times 2$ agents $\times 10$ seeds $= 240$ episodes) neither policy
exhibits a perturbation cliff. The Relational RL policy retains
$100.0\%$ coverage in every cell, its reward advantage over the DQN
remains in the $+50\%$ to $+70\%$ range throughout, and its wall-
collision counts remain an order of magnitude below the DQN at every
magnitude. This is the claim the appendix establishes: within the
tested envelope, the gains reported in Section~\ref{sec:relational}
are not contingent on the specific training-time values of GPS
fidelity, shadowing variance, or UAV actuation noise.
