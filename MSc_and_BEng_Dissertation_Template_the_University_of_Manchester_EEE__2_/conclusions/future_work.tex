\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:future_work}

Three extensions are prioritised. (1)~\textbf{Clustered layout curriculum}: the cross-layout analysis identifies bimodal sensor distributions as the primary out-of-distribution failure mode ($-14.0\%$, $d=5.33$); adding clustered and ring placement distributions to the training curriculum would directly address this boundary condition without architectural changes. (2)~\textbf{Sim-to-Real validation}: the HIL profile characterises perturbations independently; the primary remaining uncertainty is their joint effect (GPS noise, wind drift, and duty-cycle variation simultaneously), which requires hardware-in-the-loop testing over a real LoRaWAN testbed to quantify~\cite{tobin2017domain,zhao2021sim2real}. (3)~\textbf{3D trajectory and continuous actions}: fixing altitude at 100\,m abstracts the altitude-dependent RSSI breakpoint; incorporating altitude control and adopting SAC or TD3 would enable fine-grained positioning at the radial distance that maximises the Capture Effect power differential, a trade-off the current 2D discrete action space cannot express.
