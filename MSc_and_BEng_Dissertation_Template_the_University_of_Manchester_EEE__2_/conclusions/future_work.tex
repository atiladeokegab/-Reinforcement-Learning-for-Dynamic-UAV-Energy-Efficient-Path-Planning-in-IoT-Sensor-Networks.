\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:future_work}

Four extensions are motivated directly by the findings of this work.

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Multi-UAV coordination}: the operational envelope analysis establishes
    that $1{,}000{\times}1{,}000$ grids are physically infeasible for a single UAV.
    A QMIX~\cite{rashid2018qmix} or MAPPO extension would partition sensor coverage
    across a fleet, making large-scale deployments tractable and relaxing the fairness
    ceiling at $N=40$ ($p=0.129$). This is the highest-priority extension.

    \item \textbf{Attention/GNN architecture}: the fixed-capacity MLP with frame stacking
    has no explicit mechanism to encode sensor topology or inter-cluster routing. An
    attention-based policy~\cite{kool2018attention} or graph-neural-network
    policy~\cite{cappart2023combinatorial} operating over a sensor-graph would enable the
    agent to plan globally across spatially separated clusters, the precise failure mode
    identified in Section~\ref{sec:clustered_curriculum}.

    \item \textbf{3D trajectory and continuous actions}: the current 2D discrete action
    space cannot express altitude control. Incorporating altitude with SAC or TD3 would
    allow the agent to optimise its position along the radial dimension that maximises the
    Capture Effect power differential, a degree of freedom the current formulation cannot
    exploit.

    \item \textbf{Sim-to-Real validation}: the HIL profile characterises perturbations
    independently; quantifying their joint effect under simultaneous GPS error, path-loss
    mismatch, and wind drift requires hardware-in-the-loop testing over a real LoRaWAN
    testbed~\cite{tobin2017domain,zhao2021sim2real}.
\end{enumerate}
