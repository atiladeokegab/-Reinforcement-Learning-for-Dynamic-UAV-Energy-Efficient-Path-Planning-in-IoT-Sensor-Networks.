\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:future_work}

Five extensions are motivated directly by the findings.
(1)~\textbf{Richer attention over the sensor set.} The Relational-RL
policy uses a single fixed UAV query in the cross-attention layer,
which weights sensors by their relevance to the current UAV state but
does not model pairwise inter-sensor relationships. A natural next
step is a multi-query or graph-attention
pool~\cite{kool2018attention} that introduces learned per-sensor query
embeddings, providing a richer representation of inter-sensor routing
cost while preserving permutation invariance; the TSP-Oracle ceiling
is the concrete optimisation target.
(2)~\textbf{Multi-UAV coordination.} Even the TSP Oracle reaches
only $76.8\%$ NDR at $(500{\times}500, N{=}50)$ because the
traversable distance is capped by the episode energy budget. A
QMIX~\cite{rashid2018qmix} or MAPPO extension would partition
coverage across a fleet and directly address this physical scaling
ceiling; the highest-priority extension.
(3)~\textbf{3D trajectory and continuous actions.} The present
action space is 2D and discrete. Incorporating altitude with SAC or
TD3 would let the agent exploit the radial dimension that maximises
the Capture Effect power differential.
(4)~\textbf{Parametric log-distance channel model.} The Two-Ray
implementation in \texttt{iot\_sensors.py:\allowbreak calculate\_rssi()}
uses hardcoded slope constants (20 and 40\,dB/decade); the
\texttt{path\_loss\_exponent} attribute is therefore inert and the
path-loss panel of the sim-to-real sweep is a no-op. Replacing the
Two-Ray block with a parametric log-distance model
$\mathrm{PL}(d) = \mathrm{PL}(d_0) + 10\,n\log_{10}(d/d_0)$ would
expose $n$ as a genuine perturbation knob; a high-priority
implementation fix before any further sim-to-real study.
(5)~\textbf{Sim-to-real validation.} Joint-effect characterisation
under a real LoRaWAN
testbed~\cite{tobin2017domain,zhao2021sim2real}. The Relational-RL
policy, being permutation invariant, is expected to transfer more
robustly than the flat-MLP DQN to real sensor placements, itself a
hardware-in-the-loop hypothesis to test.
