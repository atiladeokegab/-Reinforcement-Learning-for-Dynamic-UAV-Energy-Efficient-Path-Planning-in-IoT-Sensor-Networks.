\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:future_work}

The principal directions for extending this work are summarised below.

\paragraph{Sim-to-Real Validation.}
The most important near-term extension is hardware-in-the-loop (HIL) deployment on a real UAV platform over a LoRaWAN testbed to quantify the residual sim-to-real gap and validate the domain randomisation regime under joint perturbations~\cite{tobin2017domain,zhao2021sim2real}.

\paragraph{Multi-UAV Coordination.}
The single-UAV constraint limits scalability at high density. Extending the quasi-distributed DQL approach of Lee et al.~\cite{lee2025quasi} with Capture Effect and ADR awareness would allow multiple UAVs to jointly manage SF orthogonality without centralised signalling.

\paragraph{3D Trajectory and Continuous Actions.}
Incorporating altitude as a control variable and adopting continuous-action algorithms (SAC or TD3) would enable fine-grained positioning that exploits the altitude-dependent RSSI breakpoint for Capture Effect maximisation.

\paragraph{Attention Policies and Heterogeneous Networks.}
Transformer-based or graph-attention policies would remove the $N_{\max}=50$ padding constraint, enabling scalability to 6G IoT densities~\cite{nguyen20226g} and heterogeneous per-sensor traffic profiles.
