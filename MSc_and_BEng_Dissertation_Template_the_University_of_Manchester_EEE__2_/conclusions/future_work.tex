\subsection{Future Work}
\label{sec:future_work}

Four experiments follow from these results.

The most pressing is replacing the masked sum-pool in the Relational-RL policy with a multi-head attention pool~\cite{kool2018attention}. Sum-pooling weights every sensor equally, whereas attention can prioritise by proximity and urgency. The TSP-Oracle ceiling ($76.8\%$ NDR at $500{\times}500$/$N{=}50$) is the target.

Even the Oracle leaves $23\%$ of sensors uncollected at that scale, capped by the episode energy budget. A multi-UAV extension using QMIX~\cite{rashid2018qmix} or MAPPO would split coverage across vehicles and break this physical ceiling.

The head-to-head comparison in Sections~\ref{sec:relational_diagnostic} and~\ref{sec:relational_headtohead} changes both architecture and algorithm together. Running PPO with the flat MLP, and DQN with the set encoder, on the same curriculum would separate whether the NDR gain at $(300{\times}300, N{=}30)$ comes from the inductive bias or the on-policy training regime. This is the most important controlled experiment that was not run.

Finally, hardware-in-the-loop validation on a real LoRaWAN testbed~\cite{tobin2017domain,zhao2021sim2real}. The Relational-RL policy is permutation invariant and should transfer more robustly than the flat-MLP DQN to real sensor placements, though that hypothesis has not been tested.
