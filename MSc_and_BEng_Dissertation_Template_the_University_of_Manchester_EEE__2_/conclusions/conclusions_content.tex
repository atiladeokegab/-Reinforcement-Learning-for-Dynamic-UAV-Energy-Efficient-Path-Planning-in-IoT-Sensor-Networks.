\subsection{Conclusions}

This dissertation coupled UAV trajectory planning with the full LoRaWAN physics stack inside a Gymnasium simulation, trained a DQN agent under domain randomisation and a competence-gated curriculum, and used the resulting framework to diagnose and then resolve an architectural limit of flat-policy learners on this problem. Five contributions resulted. (1)~A full-stack LoRaWAN physics environment (Section~\ref{sec:env}) that models the complete causal chain from UAV position through Two-Ray path loss, log-normal shadowing, EMA-based ADR, and the LoRa Capture Effect to per-packet delivery in a single timestep; no prior open simulator couples all four effects end-to-end. (2)~A three-stage competence-gated domain-randomised curriculum (Section~\ref{sec:training}) that produces a single DQN policy deployable across all $(\text{grid}, N)$ combinations without retraining. (3)~A 50-seed five-condition benchmarked comparison (Section~\ref{sec:multi_seed}) against Nearest-Sensor Greedy, SF-Aware Greedy, and a TSP Oracle, quantifying the operational envelope and the routing-efficiency ceiling. (4)~An architectural diagnostic (Section~\ref{sec:diagnostic}) showing the trained DQN produces median zero wall collisions but $100.0\%$ boundary-cell occupancy, tracing this to the absence of a set-structured inductive bias in the flat-MLP policy. (5)~A permutation-invariant Relational-RL policy (Section~\ref{sec:relational}) that, under matched conditions, reduces edge-cell occupancy from $100.0\%$ to $21.5\%$, eliminates wall collisions (mean $21.8 \to 0$), and dominates the DQN across every multi-seed scalability condition: NDR at $(300{\times}300, N{=}30)$ rises from $52.7\%$ to $97.7\%$; at $(500{\times}500, N{=}50)$ from $13.0\%$ to $56.4\%$ (all $p < 10^{-4}$, Welch's $t$-test).

The scalability sweep identifies three regimes (trivial, policy-sensitive, battery-bounded), with the feasibility boundary determined by physics rather than policy quality. The architectural diagnostic localises the DQN bottleneck to the absence of permutation invariance over the sensor set, making it a constructive finding that predicts the direction of the fix. The Relational-RL system, which combines a permutation-invariant encoder with two PPO-motivated reward supplements (potential-based navigation shaping and a cluster dwell bonus), empirically eliminates the diagnostic pathology and closes a measurable fraction of the TSP-Oracle reward ceiling. Retraining the DQN with the same cluster dwell bonus shifted NDR by at most $1\%$ across all five conditions, confirming the performance gap comes from the architecture rather than the reward supplement. The diagnostic-then-fix pairing is the principal contribution.

Several limitations remain. The boundary diagnostic is evaluated at a single $(200, 20)$ scale across 20 seeds; the scalability comparison uses 50 seeds across five conditions. The Lawnmower baseline uses 5 seeds and is kept as an indicative coverage-only benchmark. The simulation is 2D at fixed $100$\,m altitude with no 3D channel effects (urban shadowing, terrain reflection) modelled.
