Mobile UAV gateways in dense LoRaWAN networks face a collision problem: as the UAV moves,
rapid link-quality changes cause sensors to converge on identical Spreading Factors,
collapsing packet delivery in a phenomenon termed the ``SF Monoculture.'' Existing solutions
are siloed, standard path planners ignore this failure mode, and static protocol optimisers
cannot adapt to UAV mobility. This dissertation addresses both gaps by treating UAV physical
position as a direct control input for the LoRaWAN radio protocol, and by characterising the
physical operational envelope within which a single UAV can feasibly collect data.

A protocol-aware Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) framework trains a Deep Q-Network (DQN)
agent within a custom Gymnasium environment, to the author's knowledge the first open
simulation to model the complete causal chain from UAV position through RSSI, EMA-based
Adaptive Data Rate (ADR) latency, and the LoRa Capture Effect to packet delivery within a
single step. The environment incorporates the Two-Ray Ground Reflection path loss model,
log-normal shadowing, and asynchronous duty cycles, producing a realistic link-budget
constraint: sensors beyond approximately 365 grid units ($3{,}650$\,m) fall outside the
collection radius, making UAV positioning genuinely consequential. A Domain Randomisation
and Curriculum Learning regime produces a single generalised policy deployable without
retraining across grids from $100{\times}100$ to $1{,}000{\times}1{,}000$ cells and network
densities of 10 to 40 sensors.

Multi-seed evaluation (20 seeds per condition) against a protocol-blind Nearest-Sensor
Greedy and a novel SF-Aware Max-Throughput Greedy baseline reveals a clear operational
envelope. At compact scales ($100{\times}100$, $300{\times}300$), all sensors remain within
link-budget range from any UAV position and all agents achieve near-identical coverage. At
intermediate scales ($500{\times}500$), link-budget constraints become active: the DQN's
protocol-aware repositioning strategy yields statistically significant throughput advantages
over both greedy baselines ($p<0.05$, Welch's $t$-test). At $1{,}000{\times}1{,}000$, the
problem becomes physically infeasible for a single UAV: the energy budget supports at most
$2{,}100$ steps of flight, insufficient to traverse the $14{,}142$\,m grid diagonal, and
no agent achieves full sensor coverage. The key emergent behaviour is strategic
dwell-then-reposition: the DQN hovers near sensor clusters to drive EMA-ADR convergence to
lower Spreading Factors before repositioning to exploit orthogonal-SF concurrent reception,
a temporal planning strategy that one-step greedy heuristics cannot replicate. A component
ablation confirms that the Capture Effect and Domain Randomisation are individually
necessary, while the physical infeasibility at $1{,}000{\times}1{,}000$ scale identifies
multi-UAV coordination as the required architectural extension.
