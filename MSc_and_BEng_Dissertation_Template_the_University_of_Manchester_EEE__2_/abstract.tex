This dissertation addresses energy-efficient, dynamic path planning for Unmanned Aerial
Vehicles (UAVs) tasked with data collection in unsynchronised LoRaWAN Internet of Things
(IoT) networks. The integration of mobile UAV gateways into LoRaWAN introduces a fundamental
challenge: rapid link-quality changes caused by UAV movement drive devices to converge on
identical Spreading Factors, collapsing packet delivery rates in a phenomenon termed the
``SF Monoculture.''

A protocol-aware Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) framework is proposed, utilising a Deep
Q-Network (DQN) agent trained within a custom, high-fidelity Gymnasium environment---to the
author's knowledge the first open simulation to model the complete causal chain from UAV
position through RSSI, EMA-based Adaptive Data Rate (ADR) latency, and the LoRa Capture
Effect to packet delivery within a single step. The environment additionally incorporates
the Two-Ray Ground Reflection path loss model, log-normal shadowing, and asynchronous duty
cycles. A Domain Randomisation and Curriculum Learning regime produces a single generalised
policy, deployable without retraining across grids spanning $100{\times}100$ to
$1{,}000{\times}1{,}000$ cells and network densities of 10 to 40 sensors.

Multi-seed evaluation (20 seeds per condition, 30 for $N=40$) against a protocol-blind
Nearest-Sensor Greedy and a novel SF-Aware Max-Throughput Greedy baseline demonstrates
that the DQN consistently outperforms both agents. The throughput advantage grows with
network density, reaching $+8.6\%$ cumulative reward at $N=40$ ($p=0.043$, Cohen's
$d=0.54$). At 40 sensors the DQN achieves a Jain's Fairness Index of
$0.739 \pm 0.074$ versus $0.709 \pm 0.075$ for the SF-Aware baseline ($+4.2\%$), with a
$+8.7\%$ improvement in energy efficiency (259.5 vs.\ 238.7\,B/Wh). Statistical
significance is confirmed at all four tested network densities ($N \in \{10,20,30,40\}$,
$p < 0.05$, Welch's $t$-test). The key emergent behaviour is strategic repositioning: the
DQN allocates 2.9\% of steps to deliberate movement ($p=0.002$, Cohen's $d=2.1$),
reaching positions that yield $+14\%$ more bytes per collection step by inducing
EMA-ADR convergence to lower Spreading Factors---demonstrating that UAV physical position
can serve as an active control variable for the radio protocol.
