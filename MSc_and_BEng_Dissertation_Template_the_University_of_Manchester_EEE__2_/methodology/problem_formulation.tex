\subsection{Problem Formulation}

We formulate the UAV trajectory optimisation problem as a
finite-horizon Partially Observable MDP. The link-quality signal
$Q_{i,t}$ is zero for out-of-range sensors, leaving channel state
and SF distributions beyond the UAV's current footprint hidden.
The objective is to maximise total collected throughput subject to
energy, kinematic, and protocol constraints:
\begin{align}
    \text{(P1):} \quad \max_{\pi} \quad & \sum_{t=0}^{T} \sum_{i=1}^{N} D_{i,t}
        \label{eq:p1_obj} \\
    \text{s.t.} \quad
    & \mathrm{RSSI}_{i,t}(p_t) \geq \mathrm{RSSI}_{\min}(\mathrm{SF}_{i,t})
        \quad \forall i \label{eq:p1_c1} \\
    & E_{t+1} = E_t - P(a_t)\,\Delta t \geq 0 \quad \forall t \label{eq:p1_c2} \\
    & \sum_{i\in\mathcal{N}} \mathbb{I}(\mathrm{SF}_{i,t} = k) \leq 1
        \quad \forall k \in \mathcal{S} \label{eq:p1_c3}
\end{align}
where $D_{i,t}$ is the data collected from sensor $i$ at step $t$.
(P1) is non-convex: $p_t$ and $\mathrm{SF}_{i,t}$ are coupled through
the EMA-ADR filter ($\lambda = 0.1$), violating the Markov property.
Classical methods (MILP, convex MPC) cannot tractably encode this
history. Frame stacking (Eq.~\ref{eq:stacked_state}) restores
approximate Markovianity by rendering EMA-ADR convergence
state-observable.

\subsubsection{State and action spaces}

The per-step observation is
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{S}_t = [p_t,\, E_t,\, B_t,\, U_t,\, Q_t]
    \label{eq:state_vector}
\end{equation}
combining UAV position, battery energy, per-sensor buffer
occupancy $B_{i,t}\in[0, B_{\max}]$, a normalised urgency
$U_{i,t} = (B_{i,t}/B_{\max})\cdot(\text{gen-rate}_i/\text{coll-rate}_i)$
that captures imminent overflow risk more directly than classical
Age of Information~\cite{kaul2012realtime,abdelmagid2019aoi}, and a
per-sensor link-quality signal $Q_{i,t}\in[0,1]$ encoding the current
SF (SF7 $\mapsto 1.0$, SF12 $\mapsto 0.1$; zero if out of range).
Total dimensionality is $3 + 3N$. To restore Markovianity under
partial observability, frame stacking aggregates the last $k=4$ steps:
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{S}_t^{\mathrm{stacked}}
    = [\mathbf{S}_{t-3},\, \mathbf{S}_{t-2},\, \mathbf{S}_{t-1},\, \mathbf{S}_t]
    \in \mathbb{R}^{4(3+3N)}.
    \label{eq:stacked_state}
\end{equation}
The temporal window lets the agent observe urgency and link-quality
trends, inferring EMA-ADR convergence (link quality rising over
consecutive steps signals SF downgrade from SF12 toward SF7) without
explicitly tracking the filter state. The action space is
$\mathcal{A}$ (Eq.~\ref{eq:action_space}); Hover is energetically
more expensive per unit displacement but essential for stabilising
the LoRa link during decode windows.
