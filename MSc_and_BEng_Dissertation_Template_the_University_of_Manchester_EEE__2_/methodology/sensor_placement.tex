\subsubsection{Stochastic Sensor Deployment}

Sensor coordinates are sampled from $\mathcal{U}(0, L) \times \mathcal{U}(0, W)$,
modelling an unstructured ad-hoc topology. This ensures the agent learns a generalisable
policy rather than memorising a fixed map, while also naturally creating density clusters
that induce the SF Monoculture.

\subsubsection{Multi-Objective Reward Function} \label{sec:reward}

A dense reward is used to avoid the vanishing gradient problem inherent in sparse
mission-success objectives. The total reward at step $t$ is:
\begin{equation}
    R_t = w_{\text{coll}} \cdot R_{\text{coll}} - w_{\text{energy}} \cdot C_{\text{energy}}
        - w_{\text{AoI}} \cdot C_{\text{AoI}} - w_{\text{hover}} \cdot C_{\text{hover}}
    \label{eq:reward}
\end{equation}

\noindent\textbf{Data collection} ($R_{\text{coll}}$): positive reward for every
successfully decoded packet, encouraging positioning that exploits Capture Effect
and orthogonal-SF concurrent reception.

\noindent\textbf{Energy penalty} ($C_{\text{energy}} = P(a_t)\cdot\Delta t$):
proportional to action power draw, discouraging meandering trajectories.

\noindent\textbf{AoI penalty} ($C_{\text{AoI}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i \text{AoI}_{i,t}$~\cite{kaul2012realtime,abdelmagid2019aoi}):
mean Age of Information across all sensors, forcing the agent to service stale nodes and
preventing information starvation.

\noindent\textbf{Hover penalty} ($C_{\text{hover}} = \mathbb{I}(a_t = \text{HOVER})\cdot\lambda_{\text{hover}}$):
a small fixed cost applied to all action types (including movement) to prevent the agent
from idling indefinitely over a single high-rate sensor, a failure mode observed in
earlier reward formulations.

This shaped structure provides immediate step-level feedback that guides the agent
toward the long-horizon multi-objective optimum.

\paragraph{Reward scale rationale.}
Component magnitudes are calibrated so that visiting all sensors is strictly preferable
to revisiting a single node ($+5{,}000$ discovery vs.\ $-2{,}000$ terminal penalty per
unvisited sensor), and no component exceeds 15\% of total episode reward.
