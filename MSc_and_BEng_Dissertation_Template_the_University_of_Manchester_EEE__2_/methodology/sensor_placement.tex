\subsection{Sensor Placement and Reward}

Figure~\ref{fig:system_model} illustrates the complete system model
for the $500\times 500$ evaluation condition. Sensor coordinates are
sampled from $\mathcal{U}(0, L)\times\mathcal{U}(0, W)$, modelling an
unstructured ad-hoc topology. This ensures the agent learns a
generalisable policy rather than memorising a fixed map, and
naturally creates density clusters that induce the SF monoculture.

\subsubsection{Multi-objective reward}
\label{sec:reward}

A dense reward avoids the vanishing-gradient problem of sparse
mission-success objectives. The total reward at step $t$ is
\begin{equation}
    R_t = w_{\mathrm{coll}}\,R_{\mathrm{coll}}
        - w_{\mathrm{energy}}\,C_{\mathrm{energy}}
        - w_{\mathrm{AoI}}\,C_{\mathrm{AoI}}
        - w_{\mathrm{hover}}\,C_{\mathrm{hover}}
    \label{eq:reward}
\end{equation}
with four components: (i)~$R_{\mathrm{coll}}$ grants a positive
reward for every successfully decoded packet, encouraging
positioning that exploits Capture Effect and orthogonal-SF concurrent
reception; (ii)~$C_{\mathrm{energy}} = P(a_t)\,\Delta t$ penalises
power draw, discouraging meandering trajectories;
(iii)~$C_{\mathrm{AoI}} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i \mathrm{AoI}_{i,t}$
penalises mean Age of Information~\cite{kaul2012realtime,abdelmagid2019aoi},
preventing information starvation of any sensor; and
(iv)~$C_{\mathrm{hover}}$ applies a small fixed cost to prevent the
agent from idling indefinitely over one high-rate sensor (a failure
mode observed in earlier reward formulations). Component magnitudes
are calibrated so that visiting all sensors is strictly preferable
to revisiting a single node and no component exceeds 15\% of the
total episode reward; full per-term weights are in
Appendix~\ref{app:reward_scale}.
