\subsection{Deep Reinforcement Learning Implementation}
\label{sec:drl_impl}
\label{sec:training}

The simulation is a custom Gymnasium environment~\cite{towers2024gymnasium} integrated with Stable-Baselines3~\cite{raffin2021stable}. Each \texttt{step()} call runs the following sequence:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Update UAV position (or apply a boundary-hit penalty if the move exits the grid) and deduct movement energy.
    \item Recompute per-sensor RSSI using the Two-Ray model (Eq.~\ref{eq:tworay}) plus a fresh log-normal shadowing draw.
    \item Advance per-sensor AoI counters and the EMA-ADR filter, assigning updated SFs from the ring-threshold table.
    \item Resolve Capture Effect collisions across all co-SF transmission groups (Eq.~\ref{eq:capture}) and credit collected bytes.
    \item Compute $R_t$ from the multi-objective reward function and return the new observation vector.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{DQN architecture and training}

The policy is a Deep Q-Network~\cite{mnih2015humanlevel}: a 3-layer
MLP with hidden dimensions $[512, 512, 256]$ and ReLU activations,
mapping the frame-stacked state $\mathbf{S}_t^{\mathrm{stacked}}\in\mathbb{R}^{4(3+3N)}$
to five Q-values, one per action. Weights $\theta$ are optimised by
Adam to minimise the TD error against a periodically-updated target
network $\hat{Q}(\cdot;\theta^-)$. Key hyperparameters: learning rate
$3\times 10^{-4}$, discount $\gamma = 0.99$, replay buffer
$1.5\times 10^5$ transitions, batch size 256, target update every
$5\times 10^3$ steps, $\varepsilon$-greedy exploration decaying 1.0 to 0.03
over the first 25\% of $5\times 10^6$ training timesteps. Full
listings in Appendix~\ref{app:hyperparams}; convergence curves in
Appendix~\ref{app:extra} (Figure~\ref{fig:training_convergence}), and
stage-transition detail in Appendix~\ref{app:training_convergence}.

Domain randomisation covers grid size ($L \in \{100, 200, 300, 500\}$) and sensor count ($N \in \{10, 20, 30, 40\}$); 16 training conditions are sampled uniformly per episode. Training on a rotating mix rather than a fixed $(L, N)$ pair stops the encoder from overfitting to the spatial statistics of a single grid.

A competence-based curriculum controls progression to harder conditions~\cite{bengio2009curriculum}. Training starts at $(100{\times}100, N{=}10)$ and unlocks a harder condition only once NDR and Jain's index both clear their thresholds ($\ge 0.95$ and $\ge 0.85$, measured over the last 50 episodes) on every currently active condition. Easier conditions stay in the sampling pool rather than being dropped, because removing them causes reward collapse early in each new stage: the policy gets near-zero return on harder conditions and needs a stable gradient from the easier ones to update from.

\subsubsection{Reward function}
\label{sec:reward_fn}

Table~\ref{tab:reward_components} lists all reward components.
A step penalty fires every timestep; hovering adds a surcharge on top
because a rotary-wing aircraft uses more power to hover than to fly.
Successful moves earn a small positive reward, which pushes the agent
toward coverage rather than staying put. Terminal penalties fire once
at episode end and account for most of the negative signal at large
scale.

Two small auxiliary terms support convergence during domain-randomised training and are removed at evaluation. A proximity bonus $\eta\,(\hat{d}_{t-1} - \hat{d}_t)$ ($\eta=2.0$) rewards the UAV for closing distance to the nearest unvisited sensor, providing a dense navigation signal before the sparse collection reward triggers regularly. A fairness nudge $0.5(J-0.5)/N$, where $J$ is the instantaneous Jain's index over collected bytes, discourages the policy from repeatedly visiting the same sensor cluster during exploration. Both terms are removed at evaluation; the reported NDR and fairness metrics reflect only the base multi-objective reward.

\subsubsection{Probabilistic link-layer model}

Packet reception is a Bernoulli trial rather than a hard range
predicate. The link success probability is a sigmoid of the received
SNR margin,
\begin{equation}
    P_{\mathrm{link}} = \sigma\!\bigl(\mathrm{SNR}_{\mathrm{dB}} - \mathrm{SNR}_{\mathrm{req}}(\mathrm{SF})\bigr),
    \label{eq:p_link}
\end{equation}
where $\mathrm{SNR}_{\mathrm{dB}} = \mathrm{RSSI} - N_0$ and
$\mathrm{SNR}_{\mathrm{req}}(\mathrm{SF})$ is the SF-specific
demodulation threshold~\cite{augustin2016lora}. The per-step
transmission probability combines the link margin with the duty-cycle
constraint, $P_{\mathrm{overall}} = P_{\mathrm{link}}\,P_{\mathrm{cycle}}$
with $P_{\mathrm{cycle}} = 0.10$. Collection yield therefore depends
continuously on SNR margin: sensors at the edge of the UAV footprint
contribute probabilistically rather than stepping in or out at a hard
range boundary. Physical positioning is therefore a direct lever on
link-layer throughput.
