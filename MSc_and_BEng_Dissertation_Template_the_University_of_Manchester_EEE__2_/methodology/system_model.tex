\subsection{System Model}
\label{sec:env}

We consider a remote IoT monitoring environment comprising a set
$\mathcal{N} = \{1, \ldots, N\}$ of static sensors uniformly
distributed over a rectangular Region of Interest
$\mathcal{W}\subset\mathbb{R}^2$. Data accumulates in finite
transmission buffers of capacity $B_{\max}$, after which new packets
are discarded. A rotary-wing UAV, acting as a mobile LoRaWAN gateway,
is dispatched to harvest this data over a finite horizon $T$
discretised into timesteps of duration $\Delta t$. The UAV flies at
fixed altitude $h_u$ and updates its horizontal position
$p_t = (x_t, y_t)$ at each step. The LoRaWAN protocol exposes
orthogonal Spreading Factors $\mathcal{S}=\{7, 9, 11, 12\}$, allowing
simultaneous uploads from sensors assigned distinct SFs by the
Adaptive Data Rate mechanism. The UAV must therefore optimise its
trajectory to actively manage spectral assignments and prevent the
SF Allocation congestion collapse.

\noindent\textbf{Assumptions.}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textit{Fixed altitude.} The UAV flies at $h_u = 100$\,m, which reduces trajectory optimisation to 2D with fixed path-loss exponents and constant per-action power profiles.
    \item \textit{Single-channel multi-SF gateway.} At most one transmission per Spreading Factor is decoded per timestep; intra-SF collisions are resolved by the Capture Effect (Section~\ref{sec:capture}). The gateway receives at most $|\mathcal{S}|=4$ packets simultaneously.
    \item \textit{Class~A devices.} Each sensor has a 10\% effective per-timestep transmission probability\footnote{The EU 868\,MHz ISM band mandates a 1\% radio duty cycle; 10\% models effective wake-up rate $\times$ packet frequency $\times$ channel access probability. Sensitivity is tested in Section~\ref{sec:robustness}.} and Poisson data generation into a finite buffer.
    \item \textit{Battery reserve.} The UAV keeps 2\% battery in reserve for return-to-base, giving an effective energy budget of $E_{\mathrm{eff}} = 268.52$\,Wh over the episode (derivation in Appendix~\ref{app:link_budget}).
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{UAV kinematics and energy}

The UAV's state is its 2D position and remaining battery energy
$E_k$. It selects from a discrete action set
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{A} = \{\mathrm{MoveN},\, \mathrm{MoveS},\,
                    \mathrm{MoveE},\, \mathrm{MoveW},\,
                    \mathrm{Hover}\}
    \label{eq:action_space}
\end{equation}
each updating position by $\delta_d$ in the chosen cardinal
direction, or holding position for Hover (critical for stabilising
the LoRa link during decode windows). Energy evolves as
$E_{k+1} = E_k - P(a_k)\,\Delta t$ with
$P_{\mathrm{hover}} = 700$\,W $> P_{\mathrm{flight}} = 500$\,W,
consistent with rotary-wing aerodynamics (translational lift reduces
the power required for forward flight relative to hovering). This
differential incentivises efficient trajectories and penalises
excessive loitering.

\subsubsection{Channel, ADR, and Capture Effect}

The ground-to-air channel uses the Two-Ray Ground Reflection
model~\cite{rappaport2002wireless}:
\begin{equation}
    P_r = P_t G_t G_r \frac{h_t^2 h_r^2}{d^4}
    \label{eq:tworay}
\end{equation}
with additive log-normal shadowing
$X_\sigma\sim\mathcal{N}(0, 4^2\,\mathrm{dB})$. The quartic decay
enforces realistic collection radii and means small UAV position
changes have a magnified impact on RSSI.

Per-sensor SF is updated by an EMA filter on smoothed RSSI
($\lambda = 0.1$), with ring thresholds at
$-60, -70, -78, -85$\,dBm mapping to
$\{\mathrm{SF}7, \mathrm{SF}9, \mathrm{SF}11, \mathrm{SF}12\}$. The
smoothing principle follows Slabicki et~al.~\cite{slabicki2018adaptive};
full derivation of the ADR ring-assignment rule, the 6-SF-to-4-SF
reduction, and the 100\,m altitude and battery-budget choices are in
Appendix~\ref{app:modelling_assumptions}.

Intra-SF collisions are resolved by the LoRa Capture
Effect~\cite{croce2020lora}: the gateway decodes sensor $i$ if its
received power exceeds co-SF interference by the capture threshold
$\tau_{\mathrm{cap}} = 6$\,dB,
\begin{equation}
    \frac{P_{r,i}}{\sum_{j\in\mathcal{I}_{\mathrm{co\text{-}SF}}} P_{r,j} + N_0}
    \;\geq\; \tau_{\mathrm{cap}}.
    \label{eq:capture}
\end{equation}
\label{sec:capture}
Because the gateway decodes one device per SF, the UAV's positioning
controls not just link quality but the number of concurrent
receptions, up to $|\mathcal{S}|=4$ packets per timestep when sensors
are distributed across distinct SFs. This is the spectral-diversity
lever that greedy one-step heuristics cannot plan for. Full
per-timestep collision-resolution procedure, gateway architecture,
and Class~A device-constraint detail are in
Appendix~\ref{app:gateway}.

\label{sec:link_budget}Inverting Eq.~(\ref{eq:tworay}) gives a maximum SF12 collection radius of $r_{\max} = 365$ grid units at 100\,m altitude. The five evaluation grids span from trivially in-range ($100{\times}100$, diagonal $141$ units) to cross-grid traversal required ($500{\times}500$, diagonal $707$ units, nearly twice $r_{\max}$). Per-SF radii and per-grid operating regimes are in Appendix~\ref{app:link_budget} (Table~\ref{tab:grid_regimes_appendix}).
