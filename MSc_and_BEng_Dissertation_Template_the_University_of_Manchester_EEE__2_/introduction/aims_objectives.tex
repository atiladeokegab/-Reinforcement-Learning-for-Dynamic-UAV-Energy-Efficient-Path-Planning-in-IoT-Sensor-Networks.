\subsection{Aims and objectives}
\label{sec:aims}

This project develops a protocol-aware reinforcement-learning framework
for UAV-assisted LoRaWAN data collection and uses it to diagnose
and resolve a specific architectural limit of flat-policy learners.
The central claim is that, once the simulation models the full
causal chain from UAV position to packet delivery, the remaining
bottleneck to policy quality is the inductive bias of the policy
network, and that a permutation-invariant sensor-set policy
measurably resolves that bottleneck. The project objectives are:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Develop a high-fidelity Gymnasium environment coupling the
    Two-Ray Ground Reflection model, log-normal shadowing,
    asynchronous duty cycles, EMA-based ADR, and the LoRa Capture
    Effect into a single per-step causal chain, and formulate the
    task as a POMDP approximated as an MDP via frame stacking
    ($k{=}4$) with a multi-objective reward balancing energy, AoI
    freshness, and collection volume.
    \item Train a DQN agent under domain randomisation across 16
    $(\text{grid}, N)$ conditions and a three-stage competence-gated
    curriculum, producing a single policy deployable across the
    envelope without retraining.
    \item Evaluate the trained DQN against Nearest-Sensor Greedy,
    SF-Aware Max-Throughput Greedy, a Nearest-Neighbour + 2-opt TSP
    Oracle, and a 5-seed Lawnmower benchmark, using 50 independent
    seeds per condition with Welch's $t$-test and Cohen's $d$.
    \item Conduct a four-condition ablation study (Capture Effect,
    EMA-ADR smoothing, AoI observation, domain randomisation)
    quantifying each component's contribution.
    \item Diagnose the representational limits of the flat-MLP policy
    by instrumenting the environment for boundary-cell occupancy and
    wall-collision frequency across 20 seeds, and interpret the
    observed behaviour as a consequence of the flat-MLP inductive
    bias rather than a training failure.
    \item Implement and evaluate a permutation-invariant Relational-RL
    policy (cross-attention encoder with UAV embedding as query over
    the sensor set, trained under PPO on the same environment physics
    and competence-gated curriculum), reporting its effect on the
    diagnostic metrics, the multi-seed benchmarks, and the
    TSP-Oracle gap.
\end{itemize}

The guiding hypothesis is that once protocol physics, reward shaping,
and the domain-randomisation curriculum are fixed, an explicit
permutation-invariant inductive bias over the sensor set closes a
measurable fraction of the gap between a flat-MLP DQN and a
routing-optimal ceiling.
