\section{Project Management}
\label{sec:project_management}

\subsection*{Planning and Execution}

The project was planned at the outset using a five-milestone Gantt chart (Appendix~\ref{app:gantt}), covering environment development, initial DQN training, the Domain Randomisation and Curriculum Learning regime, multi-seed evaluation, and dissertation write-up. The plan was structured across two semesters with natural checkpoints aligned to the Progress Review Meetings.

The project broadly followed the original timeline, with two notable deviations. First, the evaluation scope was expanded: preliminary results revealed high inter-seed variance at $N \geq 20$, so a twenty-seed multi-condition design was adopted to provide statistically defensible conclusions, shifting the evaluation phase by approximately two weeks. Second, the SF-Aware Greedy baseline was not planned initially; it was added during Semester 2 to isolate the contribution of long-horizon planning from protocol awareness. Both deviations were discussed with the Project Supervisor.

\subsection*{Risk Management}

Risks were tracked via the Risk Register in Appendix~\ref{app:risk}. Two materialised. Training divergence on early full-curriculum DQN runs was resolved by gradient clipping (max norm 10), a reduced learning rate ($5 \times 10^{-4}$), and reward scaling. Evaluation time overrun was mitigated by restricting the grid-size sweep to $N=20$ sensors and five grid sizes.

\subsection*{Self-Directed Learning and CPD}

Substantial self-directed learning was required across LoRaWAN protocol internals and
DRL implementation, including study of the LoRaWAN 1.0.4 specification, Stable-Baselines3
DQN internals, and the primary Capture Effect and domain randomisation literature.
A full CPD Log is provided in Appendix~\ref{app:cpd}.
