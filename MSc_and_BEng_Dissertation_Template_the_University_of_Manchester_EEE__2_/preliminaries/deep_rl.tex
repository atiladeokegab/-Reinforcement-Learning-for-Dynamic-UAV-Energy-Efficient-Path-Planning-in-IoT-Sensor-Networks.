\subsection{Deep Reinforcement Learning}

RL formalises sequential decision-making as a Markov Decision Process
(MDP)~\cite{luong2019drl}, a tuple $(\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{A}, P, R, \gamma)$
with transition kernel $P(s'\mid s,a)$, reward $R(s,a)$ and discount
$\gamma\in[0,1]$. The optimal policy maximises expected discounted
return,
$\pi^* = \arg\max_\pi\, \mathbb{E}\!\left[\sum_t \gamma^t R(s_t, a_t)\right]$;
via the Bellman optimality equation for $Q^*(s,a)$, the optimal
policy reduces to greedy action selection
$\pi^*(s) = \arg\max_a Q^*(s,a)$. When the state is not fully
observed, the problem becomes a POMDP $(\mathcal{S},\mathcal{A},P,R,\Omega,\mathcal{O})$;
the agent sees only an observation $o_t\sim\mathcal{O}(\cdot\mid s_{t-1},a_{t-1},s_t)$
and must optimise the same return. The UAV task is a POMDP: the agent
observes its own position and battery exactly, but sensor buffer
states and link conditions for out-of-range nodes are hidden. Frame
stacking (Section~\ref{sec:method}) aggregates the last $k = 4$
observations to approximate the belief state, restoring Markovianity.

\subsubsection{DQN and PPO}

The Deep Q-Network~\cite{mnih2015humanlevel} approximates $Q(s,a;\theta)$
by minimising the temporal-difference loss
\begin{equation}
    L(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\bigl(r + \gamma\max_{a'} Q(s',a';\theta^-) - Q(s,a;\theta)\bigr)^2\right]
    \label{eq:dqn_loss}
\end{equation}
using two stabilising mechanisms: experience replay breaks the
temporal correlation of consecutive observations, and a periodically
frozen target network $\hat{Q}(\cdot;\theta^-)$ prevents the Q-value
feedback loop that causes divergence. Both are essential because
EMA-ADR convergence causes the same state-action pair to yield
different rewards at different training stages. Figure~\ref{fig:drl_architecture}
illustrates the three core components: RL loop, DQN network, and the
full training system.

Proximal Policy Optimisation (PPO)~\cite{schulman2017ppo} is the
on-policy actor-critic method used for the Relational-RL policy in
Section~\ref{sec:relational_method}. PPO maximises the clipped
surrogate objective
\begin{equation}
    L^{\mathrm{CLIP}}(\theta)
    = \mathbb{E}_t\!\left[\min\!\bigl(r_t(\theta)\hat{A}_t,\;
      \mathrm{clip}(r_t(\theta), 1\!-\!\varepsilon, 1\!+\!\varepsilon)\hat{A}_t\bigr)\right]
    \label{eq:ppo}
\end{equation}
where $r_t(\theta) = \pi_\theta(a_t\mid s_t)/\pi_{\theta_{\mathrm{old}}}(a_t\mid s_t)$,
$\hat{A}_t$ is the Generalised Advantage
Estimate~\cite{schulman2015gae}, and $\varepsilon$ is a small clip
threshold bounding per-update policy shift. PPO's on-policy nature
pairs naturally with the per-step observation cost of a Deep-Sets
encoder: a fresh rollout buffer avoids the stale-target pathologies
that a replay-buffer DQN would accumulate when the network changes
from a flat concatenation to a set-structured aggregation.
