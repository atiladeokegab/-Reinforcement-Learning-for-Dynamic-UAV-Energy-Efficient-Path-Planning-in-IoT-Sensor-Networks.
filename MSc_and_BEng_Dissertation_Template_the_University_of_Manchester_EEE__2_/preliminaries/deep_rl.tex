\subsection{Deep Reinforcement Learning}

RL formalises sequential decision-making as a Markov Decision Process
(MDP)~\cite{luong2019drl}, a tuple $(\mathcal{S}, \mathcal{A}, P, R, \gamma)$
with transition kernel $P(s'\mid s,a)$, reward $R(s,a)$ and discount
$\gamma\in[0,1]$. The optimal policy maximises expected discounted
return via the Bellman optimality equation; in practice the action-value
function $Q^*(s,a)$ is approximated by a neural network minimising the
temporal-difference loss
\begin{equation}
    L(\theta) = \mathbb{E}\!\left[\bigl(r + \gamma\max_{a'} Q(s',a';\theta^-) - Q(s,a;\theta)\bigr)^2\right]
    \label{eq:dqn_loss}
\end{equation}
with two stabilising mechanisms: experience replay breaks the temporal
correlation of consecutive observations, and a periodically frozen target
network $\hat{Q}(\cdot;\theta^-)$ prevents the Q-value feedback loop that
causes divergence.

When the state is not fully observed, the problem becomes a partially observable MDP (POMDP). The UAV task falls into this category: the agent observes its own position and battery exactly, but sensor buffer states and link conditions for out-of-range nodes are hidden. Frame stacking
(Section~\ref{sec:method}) aggregates the last $k = 4$ observations to approximate
the belief state, restoring Markovianity. The Relational-RL policy uses
PPO~\cite{schulman2017ppo} as its training algorithm; details and the clipped
surrogate objective are in Section~\ref{sec:relational_method}.
