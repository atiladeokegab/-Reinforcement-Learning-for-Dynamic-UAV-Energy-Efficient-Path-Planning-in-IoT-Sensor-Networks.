\subsection{Single-Episode Trajectory Analysis}
\label{sec:single_episode}

Before the multi-seed aggregation, a single-episode trial on a
common $(200{\times}200, N{=}50)$ seed (seed 423) provides a
qualitative cross-check of the six agents on an identical sensor
layout. Table~\ref{tab:single_ep_comparison} reports terminal
metrics. All reactive learners, greedy baselines, and the Lawnmower
complete the episode with coverage at 98--100\%; the TSP Oracle
achieves the highest cumulative reward because its precomputed tour
spends less time hovering over already-collected sensors.

\begin{table}[H]
\centering
\small
\caption{Single-episode terminal performance on a matched
$(200{\times}200, N{=}50)$ seed (seed 423).}
\label{tab:single_ep_comparison}
\begin{tabularx}{\textwidth}{l Z Z Z}
\toprule
Agent & Final reward ($\times 10^{6}$) & Steps & Coverage (\%) \\
\midrule
DQN (flat MLP)                 & 4.614 & 1{,}381 & 100.0 \\
Relational RL (PPO)            & 4.131 & 1{,}736 & 100.0 \\
Nearest-Sensor Greedy          & 4.660 & 1{,}381 & 100.0 \\
SF-Aware Max-Throughput Greedy & 4.643 & 1{,}381 & 100.0 \\
TSP Oracle (NN $+$ 2-opt)      & \textbf{5.842} & 1{,}680 & 100.0 \\
Lawnmower (boustrophedon)      & 3.615 & 1{,}411 & 98.0 \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabularx}
\end{table}

The DQN's higher single-seed reward than the Relational RL does not
contradict the multi-seed result: single-episode reward is dominated
by per-step hover-collect intensity on a favourable layout, while
the Relational-RL aggregate advantage only emerges once the
comparison is paired across seeds. Qualitative trajectory breakdown
per agent is in Appendix~\ref{app:single_episode_extended}.
