\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{sec:exp_setup}

All agents are evaluated on the Gymnasium simulation of
Section~\ref{sec:env}: UAV at 100\,m altitude, 10\,m per grid cell,
274\,Wh battery, 2100-step episode. Both learned policies use
deterministic action selection at test time (DQN with
$\varepsilon = 0$ and frame stacking $k{=}4$; Relational-RL with
$\arg\max$ over $\pi_\theta$). The scalability sweep varies grid and
sensor count jointly across five matched conditions holding sensor
density roughly constant at one sensor per $10^4$ cells:
$(100{\times}100, N{=}10)$, $(200{\times}200, N{=}20)$,
$(300{\times}300, N{=}30)$, $(400{\times}400, N{=}40)$, and
$(500{\times}500, N{=}50)$. Each condition is evaluated with
\textbf{50 independent seeds per agent per condition}, all sharing
identical seed-controlled sensor layouts for paired analysis.
Per-episode metrics are cumulative reward, NDR (fraction of sensors
visited), Jain's fairness on per-sensor collection ratios, and energy
efficiency (bytes per Wh). Statistical significance uses Welch's
$t$-test (two-sided, $\alpha = 0.05$) with bootstrap CIs
($10{,}000$ resamples) on per-seed deltas. A separate 20-seed
architectural diagnostic at $(200{\times}200, N{=}20)$ adds per-step
boundary-cell occupancy and wall-collision counters
(Section~\ref{sec:diagnostic}).

Four baselines are included. \textbf{Nearest-Sensor Greedy} moves to the closest buffered sensor.
\textbf{SF-Aware Max-Throughput Greedy} scores candidates as
$f(s) = (1 - \mathrm{BER}(s.\mathrm{SF}))\cdot\mathrm{payload}/T_{\mathrm{air}}(s.\mathrm{SF})$,
prioritising low-SF sensors. \textbf{TSP Oracle}
(Algorithm~\ref{alg:tsp}) computes a near-optimal visit order offline
via Nearest-Neighbour + 2-opt local search~\cite{lin1973effective},
with complete foreknowledge of sensor positions but no protocol
awareness: it is therefore a routing-optimal upper bound.
\textbf{Lawnmower} follows a precomputed boustrophedon raster,
reported at 5 seeds as an indicative coverage-only benchmark
(Appendix~\ref{app:lawnmower}). A random-walk agent is not
included because all five listed baselines already achieve $\ge 99\%$
coverage at the trivial scale; the relevant comparison is against
competent planners, not undirected exploration.
