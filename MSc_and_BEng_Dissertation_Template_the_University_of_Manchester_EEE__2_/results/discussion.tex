\subsection{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

The DQN's performance is limited by a \emph{representational ceiling}, not by the training recipe, the reward function, or the fidelity of the physics. A flat MLP is a universal function approximator and can express any deterministic policy over the observation, but the flat-concatenated encoding of $(\mathrm{uav}, s_1, \ldots, s_N)$ carries an inductive bias that makes index-agnostic set-structured behaviour representationally expensive. Within the reward landscape and finite training budget of this work, the policy converges to the cheapest stable trajectory it \emph{can} express: monotone perimeter-adjacent flight. The diagnostic-and-fix pairing reported here closes the gap between the physics the simulation exposes and the reasoning the policy network can extract from it.

The scalability sweep (Section~\ref{sec:multi_seed}) places the DQN's performance in the broader context of heuristic and oracle routing. At $(100{\times}100, N{=}10)$ all agents reach $100\%$ NDR and algorithmic choice is invisible. From $(300{\times}300, N{=}30)$ onward the protocol-blind Nearest-Sensor Greedy beats the DQN on NDR, and both it and the SF-Aware Greedy continue to do so through the sweep. A protocol-aware learned policy beaten by a method with no spreading-factor awareness signals that the constraint is routing representation, not protocol representation. The architectural diagnostic (Section~\ref{sec:diagnostic}) makes this precise: across 20 deterministic episodes at $(200{\times}200, N{=}20)$ the DQN exhibits median zero wall collisions but $100.0\%$ boundary-cell occupancy. The dominant learned behaviour is not strategic dwell-then-reposition; it is monotone perimeter-adjacent flight, the cheapest stable trajectory the flat MLP can produce from the observation encoding.

The ablation (Section~\ref{sec:ablation}) establishes the other side of this argument: every protocol- and training-level component of the Full Model (Capture Effect, EMA-ADR, AoI, domain randomisation) contributes a measurable effect. The gap between the Full Model DQN and the Relational-RL policy is therefore not attributable to a missing protocol feature or curriculum stage. The Relational-RL system also incorporates two reward supplements specific to its training (potential-based navigation shaping toward unvisited sensors and a cluster dwell bonus, Section~\ref{sec:relational_method}) that have no equivalent in the DQN training wrapper. To test whether the dwell bonus alone accounts for the gap, the DQN was retrained with the bonus added to its reward signal and evaluated on the same five conditions (20 seeds per condition). NDR at $(300{\times}300, N{=}30)$ shifted by $+1.0\%$ and at $(500{\times}500, N{=}50)$ by $-0.4\%$, both within one standard deviation of the baseline. The flat MLP cannot navigate toward sensor clusters, so the bonus has nothing to amplify; it only becomes useful once the architecture can seek out clusters. Taken together, the full Relational-RL system reduces boundary-cell occupancy from $100.0\%$ to $21.5\%$, eliminates wall collisions (mean $21.8\to 0$ across 20 seeds), and reaches full sensor coverage on every seed the DQN fails. Across the sweep, NDR at $(300{\times}300, N{=}30)$ rises from $52.7\%$ to $97.7\%$; at $(500{\times}500, N{=}50)$ from $13.0\%$ to $56.4\%$ (both $p < 10^{-4}$).

The TSP Oracle remains above the Relational-RL policy at every condition beyond the trivial. At $(500{\times}500, N{=}50)$ the gap is $20.4$ NDR points. The Oracle has foreknowledge of sensor positions and computes a globally near-optimal tour offline, while the Relational-RL policy is an online reactive controller whose aggregation mechanism is a single-query cross-attention layer. That gap measures the value of offline global planning and a richer attention mechanism, both of which are targets for future work (Section~\ref{sec:future_work}). The sim-to-real sweep (Section~\ref{sec:robustness}) confirms across three perturbation axes that the architectural advantage is retained under test-time conditions not seen during training.

Several limitations should be noted. The diagnostic is evaluated at a single $(200{\times}200, N{=}20)$ scale; the scalability sweep provides indirect evidence that perimeter-hugging underlies the DQN's coverage collapse at larger scales, but a per-scale diagnostic was not run. The Lawnmower baseline is indicative only (5 seeds). The dwell-bonus ablation uses 20 seeds per condition; a full 50-seed replication is left to future work. The Relational-RL policy uses single-query cross-attention; extending to multi-query attention with learned per-sensor embeddings~\cite{kool2018attention} is left to future work. The channel model assumes flat terrain; the sim-to-real sweep tests perturbations along individual axes but not their joint effect.
