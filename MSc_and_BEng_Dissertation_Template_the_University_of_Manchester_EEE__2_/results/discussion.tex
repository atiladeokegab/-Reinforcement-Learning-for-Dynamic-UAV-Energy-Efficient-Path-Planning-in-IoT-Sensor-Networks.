\subsection{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsubsection{Statistical Parity with Greedy Baselines}

The primary result of this evaluation is that the DQN agent achieves statistically equivalent
performance to both greedy baselines across all tested conditions. On the primary test condition
($N = 20$, $500 \times 500$ grid, $n = 20$ seeds), the DQN obtains a mean cumulative reward of
$5.58 \times 10^6 \pm 0.55 \times 10^6$, which is statistically indistinguishable from SF-Aware
Greedy ($5.60 \times 10^6$, Welch's $t = -0.157$, $p = 0.876$) and Nearest Sensor Greedy
($5.60 \times 10^6$, $t = -0.140$, $p = 0.889$). This is a \emph{positive} finding: the learned
policy has converged to the performance ceiling set by purpose-built heuristics, without encoding
any hand-crafted domain knowledge about spreading factors or sensor proximity. The DQN achieves
this through a multi-objective reward signal alone, over $3 \times 10^6$ training steps with
domain randomisation.

\subsubsection{The Operational Envelope}

Universal 100\% sensor coverage across all grid sizes ($100 \times 100$ to $1{,}000 \times 1{,}000$)
and sensor counts ($N \in \{10, 20, 30, 40\}$) establishes that policy quality is not the binding
constraint in this problem class. The UAV energy budget is sufficient to visit all sensors at every
tested scale. Performance does degrade with scale, but in a controlled, physically interpretable
way: Jain's fairness index decreases from $\mathcal{J} \approx 0.999$ at $100 \times 100$ to
$\mathcal{J} \approx 0.879$ at $1{,}000 \times 1{,}000$, reflecting longer inter-sensor transit
times that reduce per-sensor dwell time. At $1{,}000 \times 1{,}000$, SF-Aware Greedy achieves a
modest efficiency advantage ($161.2$ vs.\ $155.9$\,B/Wh for DQN, a 3.4\% difference),
attributable to its explicit criterion of routing toward the lowest spreading-factor sensor,
which minimises per-step transit cost at large inter-sensor distances. The DQN achieves near-identical
performance without this encoding, demonstrating that the learned policy implicitly approximates
the SF-routing heuristic across the operational envelope.

\subsubsection{Generalisation and the Bimodal Failure Boundary}

The domain-randomised DQN generalises across grid sizes and sensor counts without retraining,
validating the curriculum design. Cross-layout evaluation reveals a specific failure boundary:
on bimodal two-cluster sensor deployments, the DQN performs $-14.0\%$ below SF-Aware Greedy
($p < 0.001$, $d = 5.33$). By contrast, the DQN achieves a statistically significant
\emph{advantage} on structured regular layouts (grid-aligned: $+2.6\%$, $p = 0.007$;
ring: $+3.0\%$, $p = 0.002$), suggesting the learned policy is better suited to geometrically
regular deployments than the greedy heuristic.

The bimodal failure is architectural, not a training-data gap. Extending the curriculum to
include two-cluster and four-corner layouts during fine-tuning does not close the gap
($-15.1\%$ after fine-tuning vs.\ $-14.0\%$ before). The MLP policy lacks an explicit
mechanism to encode cluster identity or plan inter-cluster routing sequences. Resolving this
requires either an attention-based architecture capable of representing sensor topology
\cite{kool2018attention} or a graph-neural-network policy \cite{cappart2023combinatorial}
that explicitly encodes the deployment graph.

\subsubsection{Limitations}

Three limitations bound the scope of these conclusions. First, the MLP policy represents sensor
states through a fixed-length frame-stacked observation vector and cannot encode relational
structure between sensors; this is the root cause of the bimodal failure and the 3.4\% efficiency
gap at $1{,}000 \times 1{,}000$. Second, the five-action discrete action space restricts the
agent to cardinal movements while SF-Aware Greedy can route in Euclidean space, creating an
inherent path-efficiency disadvantage on sparse layouts. Third, the channel model assumes flat
terrain; real deployments introduce additional shadowing and altitude-dependent path-loss
variation not present in training. The sim-to-real perturbation results (Section~\ref{sec:robustness})
bound the GPS and duty-cycle sensitivities, but the joint effect of simultaneous real-world
perturbations remains unquantified.
