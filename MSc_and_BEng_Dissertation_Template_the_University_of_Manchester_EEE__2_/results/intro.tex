The trained flat-MLP DQN is first evaluated against heuristic and oracle baselines across five
scalability conditions (50 seeds per condition), then subjected to a four-condition component
ablation and a quantitative boundary-behaviour diagnostic. Chapter~\ref{sec:relational} runs
the permutation-invariant Relational-RL policy through the same suite. Diagnostic and
indicative comparisons (fewer than 50 seeds) are flagged explicitly where they appear.
