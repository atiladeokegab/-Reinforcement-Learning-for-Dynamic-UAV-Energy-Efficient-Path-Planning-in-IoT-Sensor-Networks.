\subsection{Sim-to-Real Robustness}
\label{sec:robustness}
\label{sec:clustered_curriculum}

A test-time perturbation sweep across GPS noise, log-normal shadowing variance, and wind drift (four magnitudes each, 10 seeds, $200{\times}200$/$N{=}20$) finds no performance cliff for either policy. The Relational-RL policy holds at $100\%$ NDR at every tested magnitude. The DQN degrades by at most $1$\,pp: at $\sigma{=}20$ grid units GPS noise (a $200$\,m position error at $10$\,m per cell), coverage falls from $99.0\%$ to $98.0\%$, inside the seed-to-seed variance of the baseline. Shadowing up to $10$\,dB does not hurt either policy; DQN reward actually increases monotonically with $\sigma$ because the EMA filter ($\lambda{=}0.1$) damps SF over-reaction to RSSI spikes that higher variance generates. The Relational-RL policy's lead over the DQN in reward, wall collisions, and edge-step occupancy holds at every perturbation magnitude. Full per-axis results are in Appendix~\ref{app:sim_to_real}.
