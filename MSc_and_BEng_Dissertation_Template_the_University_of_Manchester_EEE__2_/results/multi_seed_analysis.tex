At the 500$\times$500 marginal scale, SF-Aware Greedy achieves $1.3$--$1.9\times$ higher Jain's
index than the DQN across all sensor counts. The DQN performs comparably to Nearest-Sensor
Greedy at $N = 20$: both agents achieve similar fairness ($|\Delta\mathcal{J}| < 0.01$) and
NDR ($\pm 1$\,pp). At $N = 10$, the DQN's higher variance occasionally produces inflated $\mathcal{J}$
(J$=0.546$) due to the agent dwelling near a single sensor cluster — equal service of
few sensors rather than broad coverage.

The SF-Aware Greedy's advantage arises from its explicit routing criterion: it always navigates
toward the sensor with the lowest current SF (i.e.\ the sensor most recently within SF7
range), which directly maximises throughput per visit. The DQN has no such hard-coded prior
and instead learns from a multi-objective reward shaped by data collection, AoI, and energy.
At 500$\times$500, where only $\sim$20\% of sensors are within SF7 range from any given
position, the DQN's learned policy has not fully internalised the SF-directional routing
strategy in 3$\times 10^6$ training steps.

A large standard deviation at $N=10$ ($\sigma_{\mathcal{J}} = 0.373$ for DQN) reflects a
degenerate high-variance behaviour: on some layouts the UAV hovers near a small cluster and
achieves J$\approx$1.0 by serving only those sensors equally; on others it fails to find
any sensors in SF7/SF9 range and collects almost nothing. This high variance is not observed
in the greedy baselines, which follow deterministic routing rules.

\subsubsection{Fairness}

Fairness degrades monotonically with $N$ for all agents at the 500$\times$500 scale (fixed
episode energy budget). The DQN's Jain's index remains close to Nearest-Sensor Greedy across
all four densities, suggesting the multi-objective reward does not provide sufficient guidance
for explicit SF diversity engineering at this marginal scale. The observation that
SF-Aware Greedy maintains a consistent fairness advantage demonstrates that explicit
protocol-aware routing is more effective than a learned policy for distributing coverage
across sensors at the marginal operational scale. This motivates the attention-based and
graph-neural-network extensions identified as high priority in Section~\ref{sec:future_work}.
