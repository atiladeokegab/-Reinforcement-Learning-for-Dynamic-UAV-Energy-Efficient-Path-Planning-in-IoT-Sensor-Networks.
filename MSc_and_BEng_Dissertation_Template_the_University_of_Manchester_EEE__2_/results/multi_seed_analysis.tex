\subsection{Multi-Seed Comparative Analysis}

\subsubsection{Data Throughput and Cumulative Reward}

All three agents achieve complete network coverage (100\%) across all conditions; the
single exception is the DQN at $N=40$, seed 1337 (97.5\%), confirming the terminal
unvisited-sensor penalty drives full exploration.

Table~\ref{tab:reward_summary} reports the mean cumulative reward and mean energy
efficiency across twenty seeds for each agent at each sensor count. The DQN agent
outperforms both baselines on cumulative reward at every network density, with the
margin growing as the network becomes denser. At $N = 10$, the DQN leads SF-Aware
Greedy by $+2.3\%$; this difference is statistically significant
($p < 0.001$, Cohen's $d = 2.52$, large effect), reflecting the low variance of both
agents at low sensor density. With twenty seeds the advantages at $N=20$
($+4.5\%$, $p = 0.049$, $d = 0.66$) and $N=30$ ($+9.0\%$, $p = 0.021$, $d = 0.78$)
reach statistical significance. Extending $N=40$ to thirty seeds yields $+8.6\%$
($p = 0.043$, $d = 0.54$, medium effect), confirming the DQN's throughput advantage
is statistically significant across all four tested network densities.

\subsubsection{Energy Efficiency}

All agents fully deplete the battery ($\approx 268.5$\,Wh per episode), so energy efficiency
is determined entirely by data collected per unit energy. The DQN's efficiency advantage
over SF-Aware Greedy grows from $+2.3$\,B/Wh at $N=10$ to $+20.7$\,B/Wh at $N=40$ ($+8.7\%$),
reflecting the policy's ability to position the UAV at locations enabling concurrent
multi-sensor collection across orthogonal spreading factors.
