The multi-condition comparative analysis between the Deep Q-Network (DQN), SF-Aware Greedy, and
Nearest Greedy baselines reveals a consistent and strong primary result: all three agents achieve
complete sensor coverage across every sensor count evaluated. This outcome confirms that the
learned policy generalises robustly to varying network densities without retraining.

At $N = 10$, performance metrics across the three agents are near-indistinguishable, constituting
a \emph{trivial regime} in which the energy budget is sufficient for any routing strategy to visit
all sensors within a single episode. Cumulative rewards differ by less than 0.6\% and Jain's
indices converge to $\mathcal{J} \approx 1.0$ for all agents, confirming that algorithmic choice
provides no marginal benefit at low sensor density.

As sensor count increases, Jain's fairness index degrades uniformly across all three agents:
from $\mathcal{J} \approx 1.0$ at $N = 10$, to $0.96$ at $N = 20$, $0.83$ at $N = 30$, and
$0.67$ at $N = 40$. This monotonic degradation reflects the fixed energy budget: as the network
grows denser, the UAV must allocate flight steps across more sensors, reducing the time available
at each. Crucially, the DQN tracks the greedy baselines within one standard deviation at every
density, and a Welch's $t$-test on the primary test condition ($N = 20$) confirms no statistically
significant difference in cumulative reward between the DQN and SF-Aware Greedy
($t = -0.157$, $p = 0.876$) or between the DQN and Nearest Greedy ($t = -0.140$, $p = 0.889$).

At $N = 40$, reward variance rises substantially for all agents (standard deviation $\approx 1.06
\times 10^6$) and fairness variance broadens to $\sigma_{\mathcal{J}} \approx 0.07$. This
elevated variability is a shared characteristic, attributable to stochastic sensor layout sampling
at high density rather than any policy-level instability. The DQN demonstrates commensurate
variance to both greedy strategies, confirming that the source of uncertainty is environmental.

\subsubsection{Statistical Summary}

Welch's $t$-tests on the primary condition ($N = 20$, 500$\times$500 grid, $n = 20$ seeds)
yield $p = 0.876$ (DQN vs.\ SF-Aware Greedy) and $p = 0.889$ (DQN vs.\ Nearest Greedy),
confirming that the DQN achieves statistically equivalent performance to both handcrafted
baselines. The absence of significant differences, combined with universal 100\% sensor coverage,
demonstrates that the trained DQN policy has converged to a near-optimal solution on this
problem class, matching the performance ceiling set by the greedy oracles.
