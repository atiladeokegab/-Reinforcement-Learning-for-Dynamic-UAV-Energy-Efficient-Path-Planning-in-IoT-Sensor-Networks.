\subsection{Ablation Study}
\label{sec:ablation}

To isolate the contribution of each design component, four controlled ablations
are evaluated on the primary test condition (500$\times$500\,m grid, $N = 20$
sensors, 5 seeds). Table~\ref{tab:ablation} summarises the results.

Bar charts and per-seed Jain's index scatter for all ablation conditions are provided in
Appendix~\ref{app:additional_figures} (Figure~\ref{fig:ablation}).

\paragraph{A1 --- No Capture Effect.}
Disabling the Capture Effect (raising the SIR threshold to infinity so all intra-SF
collisions cause mutual destruction) reduces reward by $10.9\%$, Jain's index by $3.3\%$,
and energy efficiency by $12.5\%$. The Capture Effect is a meaningful throughput-recovery
mechanism: in the full model, the strongest intra-SF transmitter wins the channel, rescuing
data that would otherwise be lost. Without it, effective throughput drops and fairness
degrades because sensors sharing a spreading factor both lose their packets, creating
uneven collection. The increased standard deviation ($0.620$ vs.\ $0.410$) confirms that the
no-capture condition is more sensitive to spatial layout.

\paragraph{A2 --- Instant ADR.}
Replacing the EMA-ADR filter ($\lambda = 0.1$) with instantaneous RSSI-to-SF mapping
($\lambda = 1.0$) increases reward by $7.8\%$ and reduces standard deviation dramatically
($0.179 \times 10^6$ vs.\ $0.410 \times 10^6$). This is an upper bound, not a target: instant
ADR is physically unrealisable in deployed LoRaWAN networks. The EMA design is a deliberate
fidelity choice that models the slow ADR convergence of real network servers; a real
deployment would observe performance closer to the EMA result.

\paragraph{A3 --- No AoI Observation.}
Zeroing all urgency features produces results statistically indistinguishable from the Full
Model (all differences $< 0.2\%$). This does \emph{not} imply AoI terms were unnecessary:
during training the penalty acts as an exploration curriculum, preventing the degenerate
hovering policy observed in earlier reward formulations. This shaping is permanent---baked
into the learned weights---so at inference time the agent no longer needs to observe urgency
signals; the equidistant patrol geometry already guarantees fairness~\cite{kaul2012realtime,abdelmagid2019aoi}.
The policy is therefore robust to complete failure of the urgency-sensing pipeline.

\paragraph{A4 --- No Domain Randomisation.}
Training on a fixed (500$\times$500, $N=20$) configuration yields $5.3\%$ lower reward,
$1.0\%$ lower Jain's index, and $5.1\%$ lower energy efficiency on the same test condition.
The consistent degradation demonstrates that domain randomisation contributes even when
evaluated on a condition the no-DR agent was trained on exclusively, producing a more robust
feature representation by preventing overfitting to a single spatial configuration.
