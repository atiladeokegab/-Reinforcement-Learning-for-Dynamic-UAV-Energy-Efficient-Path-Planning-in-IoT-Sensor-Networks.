\subsection{Ablation Study}
\label{sec:ablation}

To isolate the contribution of each design component, four controlled ablations
are evaluated on the primary test condition (500$\times$500\,m grid, $N = 20$
sensors, $n = 20$ seeds, Welch's $t$-test vs.\ Full Model).
Table~\ref{tab:ablation} summarises the results.

Bar charts and per-seed Jain's index scatter for all ablation conditions are provided in
Appendix~\ref{app:additional_figures} (Figure~\ref{fig:ablation}).

\paragraph{A1 --- No Capture Effect.}
Disabling the Capture Effect reduces reward by $10.9\%$, Jain's index by $3.3\%$, and
energy efficiency by $12.5\%$ ($d = 1.23$, large effect, $p = 0.0005$). The Capture Effect
is a meaningful throughput-recovery mechanism: the strongest intra-SF transmitter wins the
channel, rescuing data that would otherwise be lost. Without it, effective throughput drops
and fairness degrades because sensors sharing a spreading factor both lose their packets.
The increased standard deviation ($0.620$ vs.\ $0.410$) confirms the no-capture condition
is more sensitive to spatial layout — precisely the variance that reduces the effect size
at high density in the primary evaluation.

\paragraph{A2 --- Instant ADR.}
Replacing the EMA-ADR filter ($\lambda = 0.1$) with instantaneous RSSI-to-SF mapping
($\lambda = 1.0$) increases reward by $7.8\%$ and reduces standard deviation dramatically
($0.179 \times 10^6$ vs.\ $0.410 \times 10^6$, $d = 1.46$, $p = 0.0001$). This is an upper
bound, not a target: instant ADR is physically unrealisable in deployed LoRaWAN networks.
The performance gap between A2 and the Full Model quantifies the cost of real ADR latency,
motivating the strategic repositioning behaviour the DQN learns to exploit.

\paragraph{A3 --- No AoI Observation.}
Zeroing all urgency features produces a negligible effect ($d = 0.02$, $p = 0.946$, all
metric differences $< 0.2\%$). This does \emph{not} imply AoI terms were unnecessary:
during training the penalty acts as an exploration curriculum, preventing the degenerate
hovering policy observed in earlier reward formulations. This shaping is permanent---baked
into the learned weights---so at inference time the agent no longer needs to observe urgency
signals. The $p = 0.946$ confirms the policy is robust to complete failure of the
urgency-sensing pipeline.

\paragraph{A4 --- No Domain Randomisation.}
Training on a fixed (500$\times$500, $N=20$) configuration yields $5.3\%$ lower reward,
$1.0\%$ lower Jain's index, and $5.1\%$ lower energy efficiency on the same test condition
($d = 0.76$, medium effect, $p = 0.022$). The statistically significant degradation
demonstrates that domain randomisation contributes even when evaluated on the exact
condition the no-DR agent was trained on exclusively, producing a more robust feature
representation by preventing overfitting to a single spatial configuration.
